It is always interesting to see how others play the game. You do not have to view this as a competition, you can see it merely as a learning experience to see how your stratagies fare against the stratagies of others

Specs for this month
7 Civilizations, King, Large Flat Map, Barbarian level = Roving Bands

This is another semi-crippled start for the human and slight advantage for the hapless AI. You are Napoleon of the French, banished on a tiny island with only your small but loyal city and a single carvel. Note, this is not a historical scenerio or an Earth like map but I figured it was appropriate to have Napoleon as the leader of an island stranded tribe.

Another twist, there are three barbarian cities each with one quality wonder, Magellian's Expidition, Michaelangelo's Chapel and Adam Smith's Trading Company. Rather than quietly build these wonders the played must go out and find and capture them.

Due to the fact that people have lives outside of Civ (even me... well I'm working on it anyway ) I have extended the amount of time you have for the game straight through to New Year's Day 2003. Enjoy!

This is the formula I use to rank the contestants. It is basically the same as the one used by the GOTM forum on CivFanatics, the only difference is that I include the bonus you get for conquering or sending up a space ship.

The formula to figure out NMCC score is :
 F = Final NMCC Score
N = normal score
M = maximum number of turns (2020AD being the last turn)
T = turn finished

I have attached a MicroSoft Excel program that you can use to calculate your own score (courtesy of CivFanatics), to get your NMCC score simply put in your official Civ score along with the date finished, difficulty level and map size. Download NMCC Score Calculator (16K)
Don't sweat your score too much, just go out there and crush that AI!
